Les Deux Magots is a famous café in the Saint-Germain-des-Prés area of Paris, France. It once had a reputation as the rendezvous of the literary and intellectual élite of the city. It is now a popular tourist destination. Its historical reputation is derived from the patronage of Surrealist artists, intellectuals such as Simone de Beauvoir and Jean-Paul Sartre, and young writers, such as Ernest Hemingway. Other patrons included Albert Camus, Pablo Picasso, James Joyce, Bertolt Brecht, Julia Child, and the American writers James Baldwin, Chester Himes and Richard Wright.

The Deux Magots literary prize has been awarded to a French novel every year since 1933.

"Magot" literally means "stocky figurine from the Far East". The name originally belonged to a fabric and novelty shop at nearby 23 Rue de Buci. The shop sold silk lingerie and took its name from a popular play of the moment (1800s) entitled Les Deux Magots de la Chine. Its two statues represent Chinese "mandarins", or "magicians" (and "alchemists", depending upon one's philosophical point of view), who gaze serenely over the room. In 1873, the business moved to its current location in the Place Saint-Germain-des-Prés. In 1884, the business changed to a café and liquoriste, keeping the name.

Auguste Boulay bought the business in 1914, when it was on the brink of bankruptcy, for 400,000 francs. The present manager, Catherine Mathivat, is his great-great-granddaughter.

A particularly charming district with a distinctive atmosphere, spirit of freedom and a renowned café! Its spacious seating area where the souls of poets hover, its terrace opposite the church, its waiters arrayed in black and white all set the mood of Saint-Germain. This is where you’ll experience Paris life, the paradise of words and bistros.

People come here to take pleasure in the non-stop Parisian ballet, silhouettes and city lights, to sample old-fashioned hot chocolate in little steaming pots or to chat over a dish of assorted treats after touring Saint-Germain-des-Prés.

Coffee

arabica

Arabica coffee has a caffeine content of 1-2%, lower than Robusta coffee. This coffee is grown at an altitude of 800 meters and has a very good taste. It can be said that the taste of Arabica coffee has a sour taste mixed with a characteristic bitter taste along with the color of the water like clear amber brown rather than natural black. It is the sweet smell of fruit mixed with honey.

robusta

Robusta coffee, with caffeine from 2 to 4%, slightly harsh coffee taste, round and small beans. This coffee belongs to the famous coffee today, this coffee variety is very suitable for the conditions in Vietnam, especially the Central Highlands where coffee is grown the most. Robusta coffee is considered to be very easy to grow, grows and develops well, has a high vitality and gives a very large harvest.

Moka

Moka coffee is known as the queen of coffees, with a very special and luxurious aroma, elegant sour taste suitable for connoisseurs. The seeds are relatively small and hard, irregularly shaped, yellow-green to pale yellow. The tree grows well, gives a lot of yield.

Culi

Culi coffee, also known as Peaberry coffee, has a round shape, attractive scent. The taste of Culi coffee is unique and delicate, when you drink it, you will feel the personality of this coffee along with its own features and stand out compared to other coffee beans on the market. It has a bitter taste that comes from the high caffeine content in the beans, which gives the coffee a characteristic bitter taste and the water has a wavy black color.
